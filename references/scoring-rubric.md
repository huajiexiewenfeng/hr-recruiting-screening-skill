# HR Resume Screening Scoring Rubric

Use a 100-point score. The score is an interview-prioritization aid, not an automatic hiring decision.

## Score Summary

```text
Total: 100

1. Role match: 40
2. Technical/professional depth: 20
3. Education and major: 10
4. Stability: 10
5. Project and company quality: 10
6. Risk control: 10
```

## 1. Role Match: 40 Points

Assess how quickly the candidate can take over the role described by the JD.

- 16 pts: Must-have skills and technologies.
- 10 pts: Business/domain match.
- 8 pts: Similar project experience.
- 6 pts: Responsibility depth and ownership.

Guidance:

- 34-40: Directly relevant; can likely start quickly.
- 26-33: Good match; needs some ramp-up.
- 18-25: Partial match; useful but not core.
- 0-17: Weak match for this JD.

## 2. Technical Or Professional Depth: 20 Points

Assess whether the candidate can solve real problems, not just list tools.

- 6 pts: Core technical foundation.
- 5 pts: Architecture or module design.
- 4 pts: Middleware, infrastructure, data, or system integration depth.
- 3 pts: Debugging, performance, reliability, or production issue handling.
- 2 pts: Engineering habits such as documentation, testing, deployment, and code quality.

Look for concrete evidence:

- State machine design.
- Multi-tenant or permission modeling.
- Message queue reliability.
- Data modeling and query optimization.
- Observability and incident handling.
- Deployment and rollback experience.

## 3. Education And Major: 10 Points

Score job-related education signals without over-weighting school prestige.

- 4 pts: Degree level.
- 3 pts: School strength or recognized program quality.
- 3 pts: Major relevance to the JD.

Guidance:

- For technical roles, related majors such as computer science, software engineering, automation, electronics, communication, GIS, control engineering, and information systems may be relevant.
- Strong work experience can compensate for weaker education.
- Do not reject solely for school or major if role evidence is strong.

## 4. Stability: 10 Points

Assess job duration and career continuity.

- 4 pts: Average job segment duration.
- 3 pts: Recent job duration.
- 2 pts: Career progression continuity.
- 1 pt: Reasonable transitions between roles.

Suggested interpretation:

- 8-10: Generally stable.
- 5-7: Some movement but explainable.
- 0-4: Frequent short tenures or unclear transitions.

Important:

- Short recent tenure is a verification point, not automatic rejection.
- Contractor or outsourcing experience should be discussed as project-depth risk, not automatically scored as unstable.

## 5. Project And Company Quality: 10 Points

Assess the quality and relevance of environments where the candidate gained experience.

- 3 pts: Company or product relevance.
- 3 pts: Project scale and production reality.
- 2 pts: Core system ownership.
- 2 pts: Exposure to real users, customers, or operations.

Examples of positive signals:

- Live production systems.
- Large-scale users/devices/data.
- Core platform modules.
- Industry-specific projects matching the JD.
- Clear business impact.

## 6. Risk Control: 10 Points

Start from 10 and subtract for risks. Do not double-penalize the same issue.

Potential deductions:

- -1 to -3: Key resume claims are vague.
- -1 to -3: Experience is mostly participation/maintenance, little ownership.
- -1 to -2: Recent job is very short and unexplained.
- -1 to -2: Heavy outsourcing or delivery background with unclear core code ownership.
- -1 to -2: Many required skills are only keyword-level.
- -1 to -2: Career direction appears mismatched with the role.

Risk score interpretation:

- 8-10: Low risk.
- 5-7: Needs interview verification.
- 0-4: Significant uncertainty.

## Recommendation Bands

```text
85-100: Strongly recommend interview
75-84: Recommend interview
65-74: Backup interview
55-64: Do not prioritize unless pipeline is weak
0-54: Reject for this role
```

## Tie-Breaking Rules

When candidates have similar scores:

1. Prefer direct role/domain match.
2. Prefer recent relevant hands-on experience.
3. Prefer clearer ownership over broader keywords.
4. Prefer stable project/product experience over vague short assignments.
5. Prefer candidates whose risks can be verified with focused interview questions.

## Required Caveats

Mention these when relevant:

- "Resume evidence is insufficient; verify in interview."
- "The score reflects fit for this JD, not general ability."
- "This ranking should support, not replace, human hiring judgment."
